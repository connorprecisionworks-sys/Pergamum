# Product

## Register

product

## Users

AI consultants, prompt engineers, indie developers, and non-developer founders who use Claude, GPT-4o, and other models daily as serious work tools — not experimenters. They are power users of AI, not casual explorers. They have strong opinions about prompts, hate fluff, and will notice if the UI is sloppy.

Secondary surface: brand / marketing (`/`, `/about`, `/the-science`) aimed at the same audience at the discovery and trust-building stage.

## Product Purpose

Pergamum is a community library for AI prompts and Claude Code skills — free forever. Users browse, copy, vote, fork, and share prompts and skills. The product succeeds when a user finds something useful inside 30 seconds and either copies it immediately or saves it to a collection. Contribution (submit + share a skill) is the secondary success metric.

## Brand Personality

Editorial-meets-technical. Confident, specific, no marketing fluff. The three-word summary: **precise, opinionated, open**.

Tone: write like a smart practitioner documenting their work, not a startup pitching investors. Active voice. Specific language. Short sentences.

## Anti-references

- Generic AI-purple gradient SaaS (GPT wrappers, prompt-marketplace clutter)
- Hype-first copy ("unleash", "supercharge", "next-gen")
- Dashboard-first heavy chrome — this is a library, not an analytics product
- Over-animated AI aesthetic (glowing blobs as substance, meaningless entrance animations)
- Typewriter animations as pure decoration (e.g., a typewriter on a static headline that says nothing about what cycles). Typewriters used to demonstrate content variety or cycle through real product examples are fine and on-brand.
- PromptBase / Prompthero visual language (transactional, cluttered)

## Design Principles

1. **Editorial precision.** Every element earns its space. Cut anything that doesn't directly serve the user's job-to-be-done on that screen.
2. **Practice what you preach.** A tool for AI practitioners should feel like a practitioner built it — opinionated defaults, low noise, high signal.
3. **Show, don't tell.** Let the prompts and skills speak. No marketing wrapper around the content.
4. **Expert confidence.** Designed for people who know what they're doing. No onboarding theater, no hand-holding copy, no empty "Get started" placeholders.
5. **Dark-native.** The primary audience uses Claude in dark terminals, dark IDEs. The app should feel at home there — dark-first, light as a supported alternative, not an afterthought.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum across all surfaces. Keyboard navigation for all interactive elements (voting, copying, filtering). Reduced-motion variants for any scroll-driven or entrance animations. Contrast ratio ≥ 4.5:1 for body text in both modes.
